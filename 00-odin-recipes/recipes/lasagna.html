Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish renowned for its comforting and flavorful layers. Comprising wide sheets of pasta interwoven with rich, savory elements, this culinary masterpiece is a symphony of textures and tastes. The foundation of lasagna typically involves alternating strata of tender pasta with robust layers of seasoned ground meat, tomato sauce, and creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese.

Additional ingredients such as herbs, garlic, and melted mozzarella often contribute to its complex and satisfying flavor profile. Once baked to golden perfection, lasagna emerges from the oven as a hearty, delectable casserole, inviting diners to savor the melding of pasta, savory fillings, and cheese in every delicious bite.

Ingredients

  • Pasta Sheets
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Cheese
  • Ground Meat

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the lasagna noodles until al dente, then drain.
  3. Brown the ground meat and season with salt, pepper, and herbs.
  4. Mix ricotta cheese with eggs, Parmesan, and mozzarella.
  5. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  6. Layer the noodles, meat sauce, cheese mixture, and repeat.
  7. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 25 minutes.
  8. Let it cool for 15 minutes before serving.
